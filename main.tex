\begin{abstract}
We propose a cyclic cosmological framework in which the big-bang singularity is replaced by
an information-saturation event that triggers a transition from gravitational collapse to
expansion. At the macroscopic level the transition is modelled within general relativity as
an Israel--Darmois junction across a spacelike hypersurface at a critical density, driven by
an effective confinement potential whose sign reversal at holographic saturation is
introduced as a phenomenological ansatz motivated by holographic entropy bounds. Entropy
inheritance and cycle-to-cycle homogenisation are likewise modelled phenomenologically: an
exponential damping ansatz generically drives the renormalised inhomogeneity ratio to zero
across cycles, while a first-principles derivation of the damping rate from the substrate
remains open. On the substrate side, we show that the integrated spectral weight $\rho_0$ of
the mutual-information graph Laplacian at the hyper-stiff point ($\alpha=6$) converges to a
lattice-independent continuum value ($\rho_0 \approx 4.68$, varying by $\lesssim 4\%$ across
$L=4$--$16$ toroidal lattices), supporting its interpretation as a continuum property of the
relational graph rather than a lattice artefact, and we distinguish the Weyl spectral
dimension from the fluid equation-of-state dimension. For the tensor sector we integrate the
Mukhanov--Sasaki equation through the transient confinement spike
$\rho_{\rm conf}(a)=\rho_0\,\chi(1-\chi)^8$ on a radiation background, with adiabatic vacuum
initial conditions and Bogoliubov-coefficient extraction verified by Wronskian conservation.
The computed ultraviolet tail is a clean power law $|\beta_k|^2 \propto k^{-4}$ --- a red
tensor tilt under the standard convention --- with the tilt insensitive to the spike
duration $\delta$ and the amplitude varying by four orders of magnitude over
$\delta\in[0.4,0.6]$. We further find that the $\tanh$ envelope of the confinement profile
drives the effective equation of state to $w\approx2.1>1$, in tension with the causal bound
$c_s\leq c$ under the adiabatic identification $c_s^2=w$; maintaining causality therefore
requires either a redesigned envelope bounded by $a^{-6}$ scaling or a non-adiabatic
microphysical realisation of the confinement component. These results supersede earlier
estimates of a blue tensor tilt.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Classical general relativity (GR) predicts geodesic incompleteness under broad conditions,
as established by the Penrose--Hawking singularity theorems \cite{Penrose1965,
HawkingPenrose1970}. This motivates the search for frameworks in which the big-bang
singularity is replaced by a well-defined transition. Existing approaches include loop
quantum cosmology (LQC) \cite{AshtekarBojowald2006}, conformal cyclic cosmology (CCC)
\cite{PenroseBook}, ekpyrotic models \cite{SteinhardtTurok2002}, and holographic or
fuzzball-inspired constructions \cite{tHooft1993, Susskind1995}.

We develop a cyclic cosmology in which the singularity is replaced by an
information-saturation event. The transition from collapse to expansion is implemented as a
GR-consistent junction across a spacelike hypersurface at a critical density. The underlying
intuition is holographic: as a collapsing region approaches its saturation radius, its
information content approaches the Bekenstein--Hawking bound, and the effective confinement
potential undergoes a sign reversal.

Two methodological commitments distinguish the present version of this work from earlier
drafts. First, we separate explicitly the components of
the framework that are \emph{derived} (spectral properties of the substrate, the computed
tensor spectrum) from those that are \emph{phenomenological ans\"atze} (the confinement
potential, the saturation envelope, the cyclic damping law). Second, every quantitative
claim in this paper is the output of a single, publicly available numerical pipeline
(Sec.~\ref{sec:code}); no figure or headline number is asserted independently of that
pipeline. Where the honest computation contradicts earlier estimates --- most notably for
the tensor spectral tilt --- the computed result supersedes the earlier claim, and we say
so explicitly.

\section{Pre-Geometric Substrate and Emergent Geometry}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Substrate Assumptions}
We assume that smooth geometry emerges natively from the quantum entanglement structure of
an underlying pre-geometric many-body substrate \cite{VanRaamsdonk2010}. The mutual
information between two arbitrary sub-regions $i$ and $j$,
\begin{equation}
I(i,j) = S(\rho_i) + S(\rho_j) - S(\rho_{ij}),
\end{equation}
serves as a relational metric proxy that maps spatial proximity. This mutual information
matrix dictates a symmetric connectivity matrix $A_{ij}$, from which we construct the
combinatorial graph Laplacian
\begin{equation}
L^{\rm MI}_{ij} = \delta_{ij}\sum_k I_{ik} - I_{ij}.
\end{equation}
No specific microscopic Hamiltonian or prior spatial embedding is assumed. All macroscopic
phenomenological parameters are treated as coarse-grained descriptions whose exact
microphysical derivations are deferred to future work.

\subsection{Heisenberg-Chain Benchmarks}
\label{sec:heisenberg}
To test this construction against explicit quantum states, we track the low-lying spectrum
of the graph Laplacian derived from ground states of the antiferromagnetic Heisenberg (XXZ)
spin chain \cite{CodeRepo},
\begin{equation}
H = J\sum_{i=1}^{N-1}\vec{S}_i\cdot\vec{S}_{i+1},\quad J>0,
\end{equation}
for chain lengths $N=6$--$16$, using exact diagonalisation and mutual information computed
from partial traces of the exact ground state. The full pipeline, including AICc model
selection, leave-one-out sensitivity, and normalisation robustness checks, is implemented
in \texttt{code/testing.py}, supplied with this paper \cite{CodeRepo}.

The strongest quantitative support for the construction is the correspondence between the
measured mutual-information decay exponent and the Luttinger-liquid prediction
$\alpha_{\rm MI}=-2\min(2K,1/2K)$ across the anisotropy range $\Delta\in(-0.9,1]$: the
measured and predicted exponents correlate at $r\approx0.96$, with near-exact agreement at
the Heisenberg point ($\Delta=1$: predicted $-2.000$, measured $-1.96$). The low-lying
spectrum is consistent with a smooth one-dimensional emergent geometry; however, we note
two honest caveats established by the robustness suite in \texttt{code/testing.py}.
First, information-criterion (AICc) model selection between linear and quadratic dispersion
remains ambiguous at accessible system sizes, so we describe the dispersion as
\emph{consistent with} quadratic rather than established. Second, the finite-size scaling
of the spectral gap is well fit by $\lambda_1 = a/N^2 + b$ with a nonzero intercept $b$ at
accessible sizes; leave-one-out and normalisation-sensitivity checks indicate that the
significance of $b$ is not yet settled, and the pure scaling law $\lambda_1\propto N^{-2}$
should be regarded as the large-$N$ hypothesis rather than a demonstrated result.

\subsection{Emergent Coordinate Charts on the 2D Torus}
Numerical diagonalisation of a relational kernel $I_{ij}=1/d_{ij}^{\,\alpha}$ on a discrete
2D toroidal lattice demonstrates spontaneous coordinate chart generation. As shown in
Fig.~\ref{fig:eigenmodes} for the $8\times8$ torus at the hyper-stiff point $\alpha=6$,
mode~0 ($\lambda=0$) is the flat global ground state, and the first excited tier is a
\emph{four-fold degenerate} multiplet at $\lambda=0.834$ whose span contains two mutually
orthogonal long-wavelength gradients --- the coordinate charts of an emergent isotropic 2D
manifold. (An earlier draft described this tier as a degenerate pair; the four-fold
degeneracy is the correct statement for the torus, reflecting its $\pm k_x,\pm k_y$
symmetry.) Higher modes define localised geometric harmonics with a UV cutoff set by the
lattice spacing.

\section{Spectral Foundations of the Substrate Energy Density}
\label{sec:spectral}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Canonical Partition Function and $\rho_0$}

The effective macroscopic energy density of the substrate is constructed from the
statistical mechanics of the MI-Laplacian spectrum. Treating the eigenvalues
$\{\lambda_n\}$ as the fundamental spectrum of structural fluctuations, we define the
canonical partition function over excited modes ($\lambda_n>0$) as
\begin{equation}
Z = \sum_{n=1}^{N-1} e^{-\beta\lambda_n},
\end{equation}
where $\beta$ is an inverse entanglement temperature setting the coarse-graining scale.
The internal energy density is
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm eff} = -\frac{\partial\ln Z}{\partial\beta}
= \frac{\sum_n \lambda_n e^{-\beta\lambda_n}}{\sum_n e^{-\beta\lambda_n}},
\end{equation}
and the integrated spectral weight at the hyper-stiff point is
\begin{equation}
\rho_0 = \int_{\lambda_{\min}}^{\lambda_{\max}} \lambda\,g(\lambda)\,d\lambda,
\label{eq:rho0}
\end{equation}
where $g(\lambda)$ is the MI-Laplacian spectral density.

Earlier drafts described $\rho_0$ as a ``spectral invariant'' on the basis of a single
$8\times8$ evaluation. Here we establish the correct, weaker, and well-supported statement:
$\rho_0$ \emph{converges to a lattice-independent continuum value}. Scanning toroidal
lattices from $L=4$ to $L=16$ at $\alpha=6$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:rho0}) yields
$\rho_0 = 4.870,\,4.776,\,4.728,\,4.704,\,4.691,\,4.682,\,4.677$, a monotone approach with
total spread below $4\%$ and a fitted size dependence $\rho_0\propto N^{p}$ with
$p=-0.014$, i.e.\ consistent with $N$-independence. We quote the continuum estimate
$\rho_0 \approx 4.68$ (lattice units); the previously reported $8\times8$ value of $4.728$
lies within $1.1\%$ of it.

\subsection{Physical Amplitude: The Roles of $\varepsilon_0$ and $\delta$}
\label{sec:amplitude}

Converting $\rho_0$ to physical units requires a microscopic energy scale $\varepsilon_0$
(energy per eigenvalue unit) and an effective lattice volume $V$:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm phys} = \frac{\rho_0\,N\,\varepsilon_0}{V}.
\end{equation}
Earlier drafts identified $\varepsilon_0$ as the sole free parameter of the amplitude
sector. The computed tensor spectra of Sec.~\ref{sec:pheno} show that this is not the case:
at fixed $\varepsilon_0$, the particle-production amplitude varies by four orders of
magnitude as the spike duration $\delta$ ranges over $[0.4,0.6]$, while the spectral
\emph{tilt} is insensitive to $\delta$. The amplitude sector therefore carries (at least)
two free parameters, $\varepsilon_0$ and $\delta$, and no observational amplitude claim is
made in this paper. The tilt and the ultraviolet power-law form of the spectrum are the
parameter-robust outputs.

\subsection{Two Distinct Dimension Concepts}
\label{sec:dims}

A source of potential confusion in the literature on emergent geometry is the conflation
of two distinct notions of dimension. We distinguish them here and state the fit
prescription explicitly.

The \emph{Weyl spectral dimension} $d_{\rm eff}^{\rm Weyl}$ is extracted from the
MI-Laplacian eigenvalue counting function via
\begin{equation}
\lambda_n \propto n^{2/d_{\rm eff}^{\rm Weyl}},
\end{equation}
fitting the slope of $\log\lambda_n$ versus $\log n$ over the low-lying spectrum. All
values in Table~\ref{tab:dims} use the fit window $n=1$--$12$ on the $6\times6$ torus; the
extracted dimension depends on this window, which earlier drafts left unstated.

The \emph{fluid dimension} $d_{\rm fluid}$ is inferred from the equation-of-state scaling
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm eff}(a)\propto a^{-3(1+w_{\rm eff})},\quad
d_{\rm fluid} = 3(1+w_{\rm eff}) = \alpha,
\end{equation}
where the second equality follows directly from $w_{\rm eff}=\alpha/3-1$.

\section{Holographic Saturation and Confinement Potential}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Information Bound}
A collapsing region of radius $r$ has an information storage capacity strictly bounded by
its boundary area according to the holographic principle \cite{tHooft1993, Susskind1995}:
\begin{equation}
I_{\max}(r) = \frac{A}{4} = \pi r^2,
\end{equation}
reaching complete information saturation at a limiting minimal radius $r=r_0$.

\subsection{Sign Reversal of $V_{\rm conf}$ (Ansatz)}
We introduce an effective macroscopic confinement potential $V_{\rm conf}(\rho)$:
\begin{equation}
V_{\rm conf}(\rho) =
\begin{cases}
0, & \rho < \rho_{\rm sat},\\
\leq 0, & \rho = \rho_{\rm sat},\\
< 0, & \rho > \rho_{\rm sat}.
\end{cases}
\label{eq:vconf}
\end{equation}
We emphasise that Eq.~(\ref{eq:vconf}) is a \emph{phenomenological ansatz}: the sign flip
is postulated as the macroscopic signature of a conjectured microphysical phase transition
in which the attractive gravitational degrees of freedom condense and manifest net
repulsion at extreme densities. No microscopic Hamiltonian realising this transition is
provided here, and its construction is the principal open problem of the framework.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{GR-Consistent Junction Formulation}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Causal Boundary}
The collapse--expansion bounce is modelled entirely within classical GR across a spacelike
hypersurface $\Sigma$, which behaves as a strict localised causal boundary.

\subsection{Israel--Darmois Junction}
Matching the interior collapsing and exterior expanding spacetimes requires satisfying the
Israel--Darmois junction conditions. The induced metric $h_{ab}$ must be continuous:
\begin{equation}
[h_{ab}] = h_{ab}^+ - h_{ab}^- = 0.
\end{equation}
The jump in extrinsic curvature $K_{ab}$ is related to the surface stress-energy $S_{ab}$:
\begin{equation}
[K_{ab}] - h_{ab}[K] = -8\pi G\,S_{ab}.
\end{equation}
We \emph{assume} that at global holographic saturation $S_{ab}\to0$, so that the bounce is
driven by a geometric momentum reflection across $\Sigma$ rather than exotic matter fields.
This assumption is physically motivated by the saturation condition but is not derived
from it here; establishing $S_{ab}\to0$ from the microphysics is an open task.

\subsection{Modified Friedmann Equation (Ansatz)}
Near the saturation hypersurface the effective dynamics are taken to be
\begin{equation}
H^2 = \frac{\rho}{3}\left[1 - \chi(\rho)\right],
\end{equation}
with the smooth saturation envelope
\begin{equation}
\chi(\rho) = \frac{1}{2}\left[1+\tanh\!\left(\frac{\rho-\rho_{\rm sat}}{\Delta\rho}\right)\right].
\label{eq:envelope}
\end{equation}
The $\tanh$ form is chosen for smoothness and is not derived; alternative envelopes with
the same limits are equally admissible at this stage, and Sec.~\ref{sec:causality} shows
that the choice is in fact \emph{constrained} by causality.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Entropy Inheritance and Renormalisation}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

In a cyclic universe, the absolute entropy produced during the expansion phase of cycle $n$,
combined with the residual entropy inherited from cycle $n-1$, dictates the total entropy
dumped into the subsequent bounce throat:
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm dump}^{(n)} = S_{\rm prod}^{(n)} + S_{\rm inh}^{(n)}.
\end{equation}
The dimensionless renormalised inhomogeneity ratio is
\begin{equation}
\tilde{S}^{(n+1)} = \frac{S_{\rm inh}^{(n+1)}}{S_{\max}^{(n+1)}}.
\end{equation}
If the total available phase space $S_{\max}$ expands at a faster geometric rate than the
inherited entropy across each bounce, the universe re-enters the expansion phase in an
effectively low-entropy configuration without non-unitary entropy destruction. Whether the
substrate dynamics enforce this hierarchy of growth rates is not derived here; it is the
defining assumption of the cyclic sector.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Homogenisation (Conjecture)}
\label{sec:homog}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The generation of an isotropic background from an initially asymmetrical collapse is
conjectured to proceed in two stages: chaotic mixing during the late contracting phase,
followed by holographic coarse-graining of the remaining bulk fluctuations into
$\mathcal{O}(A/\ell_P^2)$ boundary degrees of freedom near saturation.

We flag this mechanism as a conjecture rather than a result. In direct numerical tests, a
naive real-space block decimation (Kron reduction) of anisotropic MI graphs does \emph{not}
by itself reduce the anisotropy of the effective couplings; the anisotropy measure
fluctuates rather than flowing to zero under successive decimation steps. If holographic
coarse-graining suppresses anisotropy, it must do so through a different operation (for
example, spectral projection onto low-lying modes), and demonstrating this explicitly is
required before the mechanism can be claimed. Consequently, no solution to the flatness or
horizon problems is claimed in this paper.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Cyclic Attractor (Phenomenological)}
\label{sec:attractor}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each discrete cosmic bounce defines a discrete map $\mathcal{R}$ on the space of effective
background couplings:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{R}:\{\lambda^{(n)}\}\mapsto\{\lambda^{(n+1)}\}.
\end{equation}
We model the cycle-to-cycle dilution of inhomogeneity by the exponential damping ansatz
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{D}_n = \exp\!\left(-\frac{c\,N}{\rho_{\rm throat}^{(n)}}\right),
\qquad 0<\mathcal{D}_n<1,
\label{eq:damping}
\end{equation}
where $\rho_{\rm throat}^{(n)}$ is the partition-function energy density of the
MI-Laplacian evaluated at the $n$-th throat and $c$ is a dimensionless coefficient.

Two honest statements replace the claims of earlier drafts. First, convergence of
$\tilde{S}(n)$ to zero under Eq.~(\ref{eq:damping}) is \emph{generic}, not fine-tuned:
because $0<\mathcal{D}_n<1$ for any $c>0$ while $S_{\max}$ grows geometrically, the
renormalised ratio decays for essentially any coefficient (Fig.~\ref{fig:attractor} shows
$c=0.05$, $0.22$, and $1.0$). The existence of the fixed point $\tilde{S}^*=0$ is therefore
a structural property of the ansatz, and Fig.~\ref{fig:attractor} is an illustration of
that ansatz, not a derivation. Second, the coefficient $c$ is a free parameter: direct
attempts to derive it from the Gibbs entropy of the MI-Laplacian partition function do not
yield a stable value, so we do not attach significance to any particular choice. A
first-principles derivation of the damping rate is an open problem.

\section{Scalar Perturbations Across the Holographic Saturation Bounce}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Perturbation Framework}
We evaluate the structural stability of the bounce by tracking linear cosmological
perturbations in the longitudinal gauge:
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = -(1+2\Phi)\,dt^2 + a(t)^2(1-2\Phi)\,d\vec{x}^2,
\end{equation}
where $\Phi$ denotes the gauge-invariant gravitational potential. The comoving curvature
perturbation $\zeta$ is
\begin{equation}
\zeta = -\Phi - \frac{H}{\dot{\rho}}\,\delta\rho.
\end{equation}
Transforming to conformal time, the Mukhanov--Sasaki variable $v=z\zeta$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
v_k'' + \left(c_s^2 k^2 - \frac{z''}{z}\right)v_k = 0.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Effective Sound Speed and the Causality Constraint}
\label{sec:causality}
From the modified Friedmann framework,
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm eff} = \rho[1-\chi(\rho)],\qquad
\dot{H} = -\tfrac{1}{2}(\rho_{\rm eff}+p_{\rm eff}),
\end{equation}
with the adiabatic identification
\begin{equation}
c_s^2 = \frac{\partial p_{\rm eff}}{\partial\rho}.
\end{equation}
Under this identification, causality ($c_s\leq c$) requires $w_{\rm eff}\leq1$ throughout
the evolution. Earlier drafts claimed that the framework achieves exactly the causal bound,
$c_s^2=w_{\rm eff}=1$, at the hyper-stiff point. Direct computation shows that the specific
confinement profile of Sec.~\ref{sec:pheno} does \emph{not} satisfy this: solving the
continuity equation for the profile of Eq.~(\ref{eq:profile}) on a radiation background
yields a peak effective equation of state $w\approx2.14$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:weff}), because the
$\tanh$ envelope is steeper than $a^{-6}$ scaling. The causality requirement therefore acts
as a nontrivial \emph{constraint on the profile class}: either (i) the envelope must be
redesigned so that $\rho_{\rm conf}$ falls no faster than $a^{-6}$, bounding $w\leq1$; or
(ii) the confinement component must be realised non-adiabatically, with $c_s^2\neq w$ ---
a nontrivial requirement, since a canonical scalar field cannot exceed $w=1$. We state
this tension openly rather than claiming its resolution. The earlier hand-tuned
$w_{\rm conf}\simeq80$ parameterisation, which implied $c_s\approx8.94c$, remains
excluded in either case.

\subsection{Behaviour of $z''/z$}
As the universe passes through the bounce throat, $H\to0$. Because $\dot{H}>0$ remains
finite, the background scaling variable $z\propto a\sqrt{\rho+p}/H$ remains well-behaved
and the pump term $z''/z$ stays finite throughout the transition.

\subsection{Matching Conditions}
Applying the Israel--Darmois matching conditions across $\Sigma$ guarantees smooth
transmission of fluctuations:
\begin{equation}
[\Phi] = 0,\qquad[\zeta] = 0.
\end{equation}
Long-wavelength modes ($k\ll aH$) satisfy $\zeta'\approx0$, conserving primordial
amplitudes across the throat. Short-wavelength modes undergo stable adiabatic oscillations.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Phenomenology: The Transient Confinement Spike and the Computed Tensor Spectrum}
\label{sec:pheno}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

\subsection{Confinement Energy Profile}

Residual confinement energy left over from the geometric reflection phase is modelled as a
transient component in the early expansion era:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm conf}(a) = \rho_0\,\chi(a)[1-\chi(a)]^8,
\qquad
\chi(a)=\tfrac{1}{2}\!\left[1+\tanh\!\left(\tfrac{a_c-a}{\Delta a}\right)\right],
\label{eq:profile}
\end{equation}
on a standard radiation background $\rho_{\rm rad}\propto a^{-4}$, with the spike duration
parameterised by $\delta \equiv \Delta a/a_c$ (in e-folds). The functional form of
Eq.~(\ref{eq:profile}) is an ansatz; as shown in Sec.~\ref{sec:causality}, it drives the
effective equation of state to $w\approx2.1$ at the spike, so within the adiabatic
identification it lies outside the causal profile class and should be regarded as a
first-pass parameterisation whose replacement is constrained by $w\leq1$.

\subsection{Computed Tensor Spectrum}
\label{sec:tensor}

We integrate the tensor Mukhanov--Sasaki equation
\begin{equation}
v_k'' + \left(k^2 - \frac{a''}{a}\right)v_k = 0
\end{equation}
through the transient, with the background $a(\tau)$, $a''/a$, and conformal time obtained
by exact quadrature of the Friedmann and continuity equations for
Eq.~(\ref{eq:profile}) plus radiation. Modes are initialised in the adiabatic vacuum
$v_k=e^{-ik\tau}/\sqrt{2k}$ deep inside the horizon before the spike, evolved with an
adaptive Runge--Kutta integrator, and the Bogoliubov coefficient $\beta_k$ is extracted by
projection onto positive- and negative-frequency solutions after the spike. Two internal
checks validate the extraction: (i) the Bogoliubov normalisation
$|\alpha_k|^2-|\beta_k|^2=1$ is conserved to the $10^{-5}$ level at default tolerance and
to $10^{-10}$ with tightened tolerance; (ii) $|\beta_k|^2$ is stable to four significant
figures under an order-of-magnitude tightening of the solver tolerance.

The results (Fig.~\ref{fig:tensor}) are as follows. Over the ultraviolet window
$k/(aH)_{\rm spike}\in[80,320]$ the particle-production spectrum is a clean power law,
\begin{equation}
|\beta_k|^2 \propto k^{-4},
\end{equation}
with fitted tail slopes $-3.92$ ($\delta=0.4$; fit residuals $\lesssim0.04$), $-3.99$
($\delta=0.5$) and $-3.99$ ($\delta=0.6$; residuals $\sim10^{-3}$). Under the standard
convention $P_T\propto k^3|\beta_k|^2$ this corresponds to a \emph{red} tensor tilt,
$n_T\approx-1$. Two structural features are robust: the tilt is insensitive to the spike
duration $\delta$, while the amplitude is hypersensitive to it, varying by four orders of
magnitude ($|\beta_{k=80}|^2 = 2.0\times10^{-23}$, $3.2\times10^{-19}$,
$1.7\times10^{-16}$ for $\delta=0.4,0.5,0.6$).

These computed results supersede the estimate $n_T\simeq1.875$ reported in an earlier
draft of this work. That estimate is withdrawn: it is not reproduced by
direct Mukhanov--Sasaki integration of the profile of Eq.~(\ref{eq:profile}), and the
finite-duration mechanism previously invoked to justify it (a $\delta$-dependent shift of
the tilt away from the eternal-$w$ value) is contradicted by the computed
$\delta$-insensitivity of the tilt. No blue-tilted gravitational-wave background, and in
particular no PTA-band signal, is predicted by the present framework as formulated.

\subsection{Ultraviolet Behaviour and Loop Sensitivity}

With $|\beta_k|^2\propto k^{-4}$, the graviton loop integrand scales as
$dk\,k^3|\beta_k|^2\propto dk/k$: the loop contribution is \emph{marginally}
(logarithmically) ultraviolet sensitive over the computed window, rather than finite as
previously asserted. Two considerations bear on the deep ultraviolet. First, for smooth
backgrounds particle production is generically expected to become exponentially suppressed
at sufficiently large $k$, which would render the integral finite; mapping the location of
this turnover requires extending the computation deeper into the UV and is left to future
work. Second, the marginal scaling itself is a well-defined, falsifiable output of the
present profile. We therefore replace the earlier claim of demonstrated loop finiteness
with the computed statement: logarithmic UV sensitivity over the accessible window, with
finiteness plausible but not yet demonstrated.

\section{Discussion}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The framework developed here differs from loop quantum cosmology \cite{AshtekarBojowald2006},
conformal cyclic cosmology \cite{PenroseBook}, and traditional fuzzball constructions
\cite{Susskind1995}. Rather than modifying the Einstein--Hilbert action with
higher-derivative terms or quantizing individual classical metric elements, it relies on a
GR-consistent junction driven by a holographic boundary condition.

The revision undertaken in this version separates the framework's derived content from its
ans\"atze, with the following net result. On the derived side: $\rho_0$ converges to a
lattice-independent continuum value ($4.68$, spread $<4\%$ over $L=4$--$16$); the Weyl and
fluid dimensions are distinct, with the fit prescription now stated (Table~\ref{tab:dims});
the Heisenberg-chain benchmarks support the MI-Laplacian construction most strongly through
the Luttinger-exponent correspondence ($r\approx0.96$); and the tensor spectrum of the
confinement spike is a computed, validated $k^{-4}$ power law with a red tilt, a
$\delta$-robust slope, and a $\delta$-hypersensitive amplitude. On the ansatz side: the
confinement potential, the saturation envelope, the $S_{ab}\to0$ junction condition, the
damping law of the cyclic attractor, and the homogenisation mechanism are all assumptions,
the last of which is contradicted in its naive real-space form by direct tests and is
downgraded to a conjecture.

Two findings of this revision constrain the framework nontrivially. First, the causality
requirement $w\leq1$ (under the adiabatic identification) excludes the $\tanh$ confinement
envelope as written, since it drives $w$ to $\approx2.1$; admissible profiles must decay no
faster than $a^{-6}$, or the confinement sector must be realised non-adiabatically.
Second, the withdrawal of the blue-tilt prediction removes the framework's previously
claimed PTA-band signature; the observationally relevant outputs are now the red-tilted
$k^{-4}$ tensor tail and its duration-sensitive amplitude, whose transfer to present-day
frequencies has not yet been computed and is not asserted.

Outstanding tasks include: a microscopic realisation of the confinement transition; a
derivation (or principled replacement) of the damping coefficient in
Eq.~(\ref{eq:damping}); an explicit coarse-graining scheme that demonstrably homogenises
(Sec.~\ref{sec:homog}); a deep-UV extension of the tensor computation to locate the
expected exponential turnover; and a redesigned confinement profile satisfying the
$w\leq1$ constraint, followed by recomputation of the spectrum for that profile.

\section{Conclusion}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

We have presented a cyclic cosmological framework in which holographic information
saturation triggers a cosmic bounce modelled via Israel--Darmois junction conditions, with
the framework's derived and phenomenological components explicitly separated. The
established results are: (i) continuum convergence of the MI-Laplacian spectral weight,
$\rho_0\approx4.68$, across lattice sizes $L=4$--$16$; (ii) a computed tensor spectrum for
the transient confinement spike --- a validated $k^{-4}$ ultraviolet tail corresponding to
a red tilt, insensitive to the spike duration in slope but spanning four orders of
magnitude in amplitude over $\delta\in[0.4,0.6]$ --- superseding and withdrawing the
earlier blue-tilt estimate $n_T\simeq1.875$; (iii) a clean distinction between the Weyl
spectral dimension and the fluid equation-of-state dimension, with the fit window stated;
(iv) a causality constraint on the confinement sector: the present $\tanh$ envelope yields
$w\approx2.1>1$ and must be replaced by a profile bounded by $a^{-6}$ decay or by a
non-adiabatic realisation; and (v) genericity of the cyclic attractor under the exponential
damping ansatz, whose rate remains a free parameter. The framework's open problems ---
microphysical derivations of the confinement transition, the damping rate, the junction
condition, and the homogenisation mechanism --- are stated as such.
