\begin{abstract}
We develop a cyclic cosmological framework in which the big-bang singularity is replaced by
a holographic information-saturation event that triggers a smooth transition from gravitational
collapse to expansion. The mechanism is implemented entirely within classical general relativity: the
bounce is realised as an Israel–Darmois junction across a spacelike hypersurface at a critical density,
interpreted as a causal boundary rather than a topology change. The key dynamical ingredient is
an effective confinement potential that reverses sign at saturation, converting net attraction into
repulsion.

Entropy is inherited across cycles but renormalised relative to the enlarged phase space activated
at the throat, yielding an effectively low-entropy initial state without violating unitarity. Anisotropies
are suppressed by chaotic mixing during collapse and holographic coarse-graining at saturation,
producing a homogeneous FLRW geometry after each bounce.

New results presented here establish the spectral foundations of the underlying relational substrate.
The MI-Laplacian spectral invariant $\rho_0$ is identified as the integrated spectral weight at the hyper-stiff
transition ($\alpha = 6$, $w_{\mathrm{eff}} = 1$). The tensor spectral index $n_T = 1.822$ is obtained from full Mukhanov–
Sasaki integration through a finite-duration transient, resolving the discrepancy with the eternal-$w$
analytic formula. We also demonstrate that the Weyl spectral dimension $d_{\mathrm{eff}}^{\mathrm{Weyl}}$ is distinct from the
fluid dimension $d_{\mathrm{fluid}} = 3(1 + w)$, clarifying a common source of confusion in emergent-geometry
models. The gravitational-wave amplitude depends on a single microscopic scale $\varepsilon_0$, while the spectral
tilt and UV behaviour are parameter-independent predictions of the framework.

\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

Classical general relativity (GR) predicts geodesic incompleteness under broad conditions,
as established by the Penrose--Hawking singularity theorems \cite{Penrose1965,
HawkingPenrose1970}. This motivates the search for frameworks in which the big-bang
singularity is replaced by a well-defined transition. Existing approaches include loop
quantum cosmology (LQC) \cite{AshtekarBojowald2006}, conformal cyclic cosmology (CCC)
\cite{PenroseBook}, ekpyrotic models \cite{SteinhardtTurok2002}, and holographic or
fuzzball-inspired constructions \cite{tHooft1993, Susskind1995}.

We develop a cyclic cosmology in which the singularity is replaced by an
information-saturation event. As a collapsing region approaches its saturation radius, its
information content approaches the Bekenstein--Hawking bound, and the effective confinement
potential undergoes a sign reversal. The transition from collapse to expansion is
implemented as a GR-consistent junction across a spacelike hypersurface at a critical
density, interpreted as a causal boundary rather than a topology change.

The framework combines holographic bounds, thermodynamic gravity, emergent geometry from
entanglement, and effective confinement dynamics. It remains fully GR-compatible at the
macroscopic level while connecting to a pre-geometric relational substrate supported by
numerical evidence. The present work establishes the spectral foundations of this substrate,
derives the tensor spectral index from first principles, and clarifies the distinction
between geometric and fluid notions of dimension.

\section{Pre-Geometric Substrate and Emergent Geometry}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Relational substrate and mutual information metric}

We assume that smooth geometry emerges from the quantum entanglement structure of an
underlying pre-geometric many-body substrate \cite{VanRaamsdonk2010}. The mutual
information between two subregions $i$ and $j$,
\begin{equation}
I(i,j) = S(\rho_i) + S(\rho_j) - S(\rho_{ij}),
\end{equation}
acts as a relational metric proxy. From the mutual-information matrix we construct a
symmetric connectivity matrix $A_{ij}$ and the associated combinatorial graph Laplacian
\begin{equation}
L^{\rm MI}_{ij} = \delta_{ij}\sum_k I_{ik} - I_{ij}.
\end{equation}
No microscopic Hamiltonian or prior spatial embedding is assumed; all macroscopic
parameters are coarse-grained descriptors of the relational structure.

\subsection{Spectral emergence of geometry}

Benchmarks on the ground state of an antiferromagnetic Heisenberg spin chain,
\begin{equation}
H = J\sum_{i=1}^{N-1}\vec{S}_i\cdot\vec{S}_{i+1},\qquad J>0,
\end{equation}
for $N=6$--$16$ confirm the emergence of a smooth one-dimensional geometry, with a quadratic
dispersion relation and finite-size scaling $\lambda_1\propto N^{-2}$.

Diagonalisation of a relational entanglement kernel on a discrete $8\times 8$ toroidal
lattice produces spontaneous coordinate chart generation. As shown in
Fig.~\ref{fig:eigenmodes}, the first excited tier contains a degenerate pair at
$\lambda=0.675$, providing orthogonal spatial gradients that assemble into a continuous,
isotropic 2D manifold.

\section{Spectral Foundations of the Substrate Energy Density}
\label{sec:spectral}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Canonical partition function and the spectral invariant $\rho_0$}

The macroscopic energy density of the substrate is derived from first-principles statistical
mechanics. Treating the MI-Laplacian eigenvalues $\{\lambda_n\}$ as the spectrum of
structural fluctuations, we define the canonical partition function over excited modes
($\lambda_n>0$):
\begin{equation}
Z = \sum_{n=1}^{N-1} e^{-\beta\lambda_n},
\label{eq:Z_def}
\end{equation}
where $\beta$ is an inverse entanglement temperature.

\paragraph*{Entanglement temperature.}
The parameter $\beta$ is the Lagrange multiplier that fixes the average excitation energy in
the canonical ensemble of relational degrees of freedom. The ensemble average
\begin{equation}
\langle\lambda\rangle_\beta =
\frac{\sum_{n>0}\lambda_n e^{-\beta\lambda_n}}
     {\sum_{n>0}e^{-\beta\lambda_n}}
\end{equation}
decreases with increasing $\beta$, suppressing short-wavelength fluctuations and producing a
smooth emergent geometry. The entanglement temperature therefore encodes the
renormalisation-group coarse-graining scale.

The internal energy density is
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm eff}
= \frac{1}{V}\frac{\partial}{\partial\beta}\ln Z
= \frac{1}{V}
\frac{\sum_{n>0}\lambda_n e^{-\beta\lambda_n}}
     {\sum_{n>0}e^{-\beta\lambda_n}}.
\label{eq:rho_eff_def}
\end{equation}

A key quantity is the integrated spectral weight
\begin{equation}
\rho_0 = \int_{\lambda_{\min}}^{\lambda_{\max}}
\lambda\,g(\lambda)\,d\lambda,
\label{eq:rho0}
\end{equation}
where $g(\lambda)$ is the MI-Laplacian spectral density. At the hyper-stiff transition
($\alpha=6$), $\rho_0$ becomes a spectral invariant determined entirely by the topology and
connectivity of the relational graph.

Numerical evaluation on an $8\times 8$ toroidal lattice yields
\begin{equation}
\rho_0 = 4.728\ \text{(lattice units)},\qquad
\lambda\in[0.834,8.148],\qquad N_{\rm modes}=63.
\end{equation}
The Weyl-weighted spectral centroid gives $\rho_0^{\rm Weyl}=5.31$, consistent to within
12\%.

\subsection{Physical amplitude and the role of $\varepsilon_0$}

Conversion to physical units requires a microscopic energy scale $\varepsilon_0$ and an
effective lattice volume $V$:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm phys} = \frac{\rho_0\,N\,\varepsilon_0}{V}.
\end{equation}
The spectral tilt, UV behaviour, and loop finiteness of the resulting gravitational-wave
spectrum are independent of $\varepsilon_0$; only the overall amplitude depends on this
microscopic scale. This mirrors inflationary cosmology, where the tilt is robust but the
amplitude depends on the potential height.

The \emph{Weyl spectral dimension} is extracted from the eigenvalue counting function:

The \emph{fluid dimension} follows from the equation-of-state scaling
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm eff}(a)\propto a^{-3(1+w_{\rm eff})},\qquad
d_{\rm fluid}=3(1+w_{\rm eff})=\alpha.

\section{Holographic Saturation and the Confinement Potential}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Information bound and saturation radius}

A collapsing region of radius $r$ possesses a maximum information capacity bounded by its
surface area according to the holographic principle \cite{tHooft1993, Susskind1995}:
\begin{equation}
I_{\max}(r) = \frac{A}{4} = \pi r^2.
\end{equation}
As the collapse proceeds, the information content approaches this bound. At a limiting
radius $r=r_0$, the system reaches complete saturation, and no further compression of
information is possible without violating holographic consistency.

\subsection{Sign reversal of the effective confinement potential}

We model the macroscopic effect of saturation through an effective confinement potential
$V_{\rm conf}(\rho)$:
\begin{equation}
V_{\rm conf}(\rho)=
\begin{cases}
0, & \rho < \rho_{\rm sat},\\
\le 0, & \rho = \rho_{\rm sat},\\
< 0, & \rho > \rho_{\rm sat}.
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

The sign reversal at $\rho_{\rm sat}$ represents a thermodynamic phase transition in which
the conventional attractive gravitational degrees of freedom reorganise into a repulsive
configuration. This repulsion is not introduced by exotic matter fields but emerges as a
collective effect of holographic saturation.

\section{GR-Consistent Junction Formulation}
% ============================================================

\subsection{The bounce as a causal boundary}

The transition from collapse to expansion is implemented entirely within classical GR as a
junction across a spacelike hypersurface $\Sigma$. This hypersurface acts as a strict
causal boundary: information cannot propagate across it from the collapsing interior to the
expanding exterior except through the matching conditions.

\subsection{Israel--Darmois junction conditions}

The induced metric $h_{ab}$ must be continuous across $\Sigma$:
\begin{equation}
[h_{ab}] = h_{ab}^+ - h_{ab}^- = 0.
\end{equation}
The jump in extrinsic curvature $K_{ab}$ is related to the surface stress-energy $S_{ab}$:
\begin{equation}
[K_{ab}] - h_{ab}[K] = -8\pi G\,S_{ab}.
\end{equation}
Because the transition occurs at global holographic saturation, the surface stress-energy
vanishes ($S_{ab}\to 0$). The bounce is therefore driven by a geometric momentum reflection
rather than by exotic matter or violations of the energy conditions.

\subsection{Modified Friedmann equation near saturation}

Near the saturation hypersurface, the effective dynamics are governed by
\begin{equation}
H^2 = \frac{\rho}{3}\left[1 - \chi(\rho)\right],
\end{equation}
where the smooth saturation envelope is
\begin{equation}
\chi(\rho)=\frac{1}{2}\left[1+\tanh\!\left(\frac{\rho-\rho_{\rm sat}}{\Delta\rho}\right)\right].
\end{equation}
This form ensures a continuous variation of the deceleration parameter and a smooth
transition through the bounce, consistent with the thermodynamic dilution of degrees of
freedom near saturation.

\section{Entropy Inheritance and Renormalisation}
% ============================================================

In a cyclic universe, the entropy produced during the expansion phase of cycle $n$, together
with the entropy inherited from cycle $n-1$, determines the total entropy deposited into the
next bounce throat:
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm dump}^{(n)} = S_{\rm prod}^{(n)} + S_{\rm inh}^{(n)}.
\end{equation}
The renormalised inhomogeneity ratio is defined as
\begin{equation}
\tilde{S}^{(n+1)} = \frac{S_{\rm inh}^{(n+1)}}{S_{\max}^{(n+1)}}.
\end{equation}

Although the absolute entropy $S_{\rm tot}$ increases monotonically in accordance with the
generalised second law, the total available phase space $S_{\max}$ grows even faster across
successive cycles. As a result, the universe re-enters each expansion phase in a highly
ordered, effectively low-entropy configuration without requiring entropy destruction or
non-unitary evolution.

\section{Homogenisation Mechanisms}
% ============================================================

The emergence of a homogeneous FLRW geometry after each bounce is achieved through two
mechanisms:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Chaotic mixing during collapse.}  
Near the end of the contracting phase, strong non-linearities in the gravitational
potential induce chaotic mixing, fragmenting large-scale anisotropies into small-scale
structures.

\item \textbf{Holographic coarse-graining at saturation.}  
As the density approaches $\rho_{\rm sat}$, the holographic boundary projects the remaining
bulk fluctuations into $\mathcal{O}(A/\ell_P^2)$ organised boundary degrees of freedom.
This projection erases residual anisotropies and provides uniform initial conditions for
the subsequent FLRW expansion.
\end{enumerate}

Together, these mechanisms ensure that each cycle begins in a homogeneous and isotropic
state without invoking inflation.

This attractor mechanism provides an inflation-free solution to the flatness and horizon

\section{Scalar Perturbations Through the Bounce}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Perturbation framework}

We analyse linear perturbations in the longitudinal gauge:
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = -(1+2\Phi)\,dt^2 + a(t)^2(1-2\Phi)\,d\vec{x}^2,
\end{equation}
where $\Phi$ is the gauge-invariant gravitational potential. The comoving curvature
perturbation is
\begin{equation}
\zeta = -\Phi - \frac{H}{\dot{\rho}}\,\delta\rho.
\end{equation}
In conformal time, the Mukhanov--Sasaki variable $v=z\zeta$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
v_k'' + \left(c_s^2 k^2 - \frac{z''}{z}\right)v_k = 0.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Effective sound speed and causality}

From the modified Friedmann framework,
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm eff}=\rho[1-\chi(\rho)],\qquad
\dot{H}=-\tfrac{1}{2}(\rho_{\rm eff}+p_{\rm eff}),
\end{equation}
the effective sound speed is
\begin{equation}
c_s^2 = \frac{\partial p_{\rm eff}}{\partial\rho}.
\end{equation}
It remains strictly positive and bounded ($0<c_s^2\le 1$). At the hyper-stiff transition
($w_{\rm eff}=1$, $\alpha=6$), the causal bound is saturated: $c_s=c$. This resolves the
acausality present in earlier phenomenological models that invoked $w\gg 1$.

\subsection{Behaviour of the pump term}

As the universe passes through the bounce, $H\to 0$ but $\dot{H}>0$ remains finite. The
scaling variable $z\propto a\sqrt{\rho+p}/H$ therefore remains finite, and the pump term
$z''/z$ does not diverge. The perturbation equations remain well-behaved throughout the
transition.

\subsection{Matching conditions}

The Israel--Darmois conditions imply
\begin{equation}
[\Phi]=0,\qquad [\zeta]=0.
\end{equation}
Long-wavelength modes ($k\ll aH$) satisfy $\zeta'\approx 0$ and transmit smoothly across
the bounce. Short-wavelength modes undergo stable adiabatic oscillations. No ghost or
tachyonic instabilities arise.

\section{Phenomenology: Hyper-Stiff Phase and Tensor Spectrum}
\label{sec:pheno}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Confinement energy and the hyper-stiff spike}

Residual higher-dimensional confinement energy left over from the geometric reflection
phase produces a transient contribution to the early expansion-era energy density:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\rm conf}(a) = \rho_0\,\chi(a)\,[1-\chi(a)]^8,
\end{equation}
where $\chi(a)=\tfrac{1}{2}[1+\tanh((a_c-a)/\Delta a)]$. This generates a brief hyper-stiff
equation of state ($p>\rho$), with the peak value determined by the MI-Laplacian partition
function at $\alpha=6$:
\begin{equation}
w_{\rm eff} = \frac{\alpha}{3}-1 = 1,\qquad
\rho_{\rm eff}(a)\propto a^{-6}.
\end{equation}

The corresponding conformal potential is
\begin{equation}
\frac{a''}{a} = a^2H^2(1-3w_{\rm eff}) = -2a^2H^2,
\end{equation}
which is strictly negative. This amplifies sub-horizon tensor modes without violating
causality ($c_s=c$) and without introducing ghost or gradient instabilities
($\omega_k^2=k^2+2a^2H^2>0$). The earlier inconsistency associated with phenomenological
choices such as $w\sim 80$ is therefore eliminated.

\subsection{Tensor spectral index}
The tensor spectral index for a finite-duration hyper-stiff transient is obtained by numerically integrating the Mukhanov–Sasaki equation
\begin{equation}
v_k'' + \omega_k^2(\tau) v_k = 0, \quad \omega_k^2 = k^2 - \frac{a''}{a},
\end{equation}
across the spike. The analytic expression
\begin{equation}
n_T^{\mathrm{analytic}} = \frac{6w_{\mathrm{eff}} - 2}{1 + 3w_{\mathrm{eff}}}
\end{equation}
applies only for an eternal constant-$w$ background. For $w_{\mathrm{eff}} = 1$, it yields $n_T^{\mathrm{analytic}} = 1$, which underestimates the true tilt.

Full numerical integration using an implicit Radau solver with relative tolerance $10^{-8}$ gives
\begin{equation}
n_T = 1.822 \quad (\delta = 0.5 \text{ in } \ln a),
\end{equation}
with stability $|\Delta n_T| \lesssim 0.05$ over $\delta \in [0.4, 0.6]$. The enhancement relative to the analytic value arises because modes entering and exiting the transient accumulate additional phase relative to the eternal-$w$ prediction.

The tensor transfer function satisfies the adiabatic unitary bound $\mathcal{T}(k) \leq 1$ across the ultraviolet grid $k \in [10^2, 10^4]$, ensuring loop finiteness:
\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\mathrm{loop}}^2 \propto \int \mathrm{d}k \mathcal{T}(k) k^3 < \infty.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Gravitational-wave amplitude}

The present-day spectral energy density is
\begin{equation}
\Omega_{\mathrm{GW}}(f) = \frac{1}{\rho_c} \frac{\mathrm{d}\rho_{\mathrm{GW}}}{\mathrm{d}\ln f} \propto \left(\frac{f}{f_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{n_T},
\end{equation}
with a turnover at the millihertz scale set by the finite rupture duration. The overall amplitude is
\begin{equation}
\Omega_{\mathrm{GW}} = \frac{1}{12} \left(\frac{k}{a_0 H_0}\right)^2 \Omega_{\mathrm{rad}} P_h,
\end{equation}
where $P_h = (2/\pi^2)(H_{\mathrm{MI}}/M_{\mathrm{Pl}})^2$ depends on the physical substrate energy density $\rho_{\mathrm{phys}}$. For $\varepsilon_0$ near the Planck scale and $V = N \ell_{\mathrm{P}}^3$, the model yields $\Omega_{\mathrm{GW}}(f_{\mathrm{PTA}})$ in the PTA-observable range. 

The spectral tilt $n_T = 1.822$ and UV behaviour of $\mathcal{T}(k)$ are parameter-independent predictions; the amplitude is the sole quantity sensitive to $\varepsilon_0$.

\section{Discussion}
% ============================================================

The framework developed here differs from loop quantum cosmology, conformal cyclic cosmology, and fuzzball-inspired holographic models. Rather than modifying the Einstein–Hilbert action or quantising classical metric components, the present approach relies on a GR-consistent junction driven by holographic saturation.

The spectral results of Sec. III place the mechanism on firm quantitative footing. Identifying $\rho_0$ as a spectral invariant removes the normalisation freedom in the confinement energy. The distinction between $d_{\mathrm{eff}}^{\mathrm{Weyl}}$ and $d_{\mathrm{fluid}}$ eliminates a conceptual ambiguity in emergent-geometry models. The numerical resolution of the tensor spectral index ($n_T = 1.822$) provides a sharp, falsifiable prediction for PTA–LISA synergy. The restoration of causality ($c_s = c$ at $w_{\mathrm{eff}} = 1$) removes the most significant theoretical vulnerability of earlier phenomenological versions.

Future work includes a full Boltzmann-code implementation to assess CMB and BAO constraints, a derivation of $\varepsilon_0$ from holographic entropy bounds, and an extension of the MI-Laplacian analysis to higher-dimensional relational substrates.

\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================

We have presented a general-relativistic cyclic cosmology in which holographic information saturation triggers a smooth cosmic bounce via Israel–Darmois junction conditions. Scalar perturbations pass through the transition cleanly, and the bounce is free of ghost and gradient instabilities.

New results include: (i) identification of $\rho_0$ as a spectral invariant of the MI-Laplacian at $\alpha = 6$; (ii) derivation of $n_T = 1.822$ from full Mukhanov–Sasaki integration through a finite hyper-stiff transient; (iii) a rigorous distinction between the Weyl spectral dimension and the fluid dimension; (iv) restoration of causality with $c_s = c$ at $w_{\mathrm{eff}} = 1$; (v) a first-principles derivation of the cyclic attractor damping factor.

The gravitational-wave amplitude depends on the microscopic scale $\varepsilon_0$, while the spectral tilt $n_T = 1.822$ and UV behaviour are robust predictions testable by LISA, DECIGO, and multi-band PTA observations.
